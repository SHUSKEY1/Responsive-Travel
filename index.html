Travel to the Enchanting Islands of the Galapagos

The Galapagoes Islands is the foremost destination for wile-life viewing. The islands are formed by an isolated group of volcanoes, consisting of shield volcanoes and lava plateaus. The isolated terrian of the island chains is home to a variety of plant and animals found nowhere else in the world.

The remote nature of the islands allow guests to experince nature in a completly relaxed setting.

The Blue-Footed Booby is one of the most infamous inhabitants of the Galapagos Islands. A favorite of many Wildlife tourists, roughly half of the Blue-Footed Booby's population can be found on the Galapagos Islands.

Blue-Footed Bobby Facts:

  1. Male and Female Blue-Fooded Bobbies have different pupil sizes


  2. The Blue-Footed Bobbies foot color becomes blue as they mature. The color of the Blue-Fooded Bobbies feet are from a diet rich in carotenoids.


  3. Female Blue-Fooded Boobies have bluer feet compared to their male counter parts.


  4. The Blue-Fooded Booby does not make a nest, instead they lay their eggs on the bare ground.


  5. To survive in a hot environment the Blue-Footed Booby can flutter the skin of their throats by rapidly vibrating bones located in their throats. This vibration creates an evaporative cooling through their open mouth.
